## Executive Summary

MathAthlone is a real-time competitive math platform built on NC Standard Course
of Study curriculum, targeting every K-12 student in North Carolina. The platform
operates as "Chess.com for math" — ELO ratings, league brackets, and a championship
path from classroom to state level.

This plan outlines a phased sponsorship strategy to fund platform growth, pilot
expansion, and statewide reach over 36 months, beginning from Mpingo Systems LLC's
current pre-pilot stage.

## Strategies

### Strategy 1 — Build Credibility Before Applying
No grant application goes out without:
- Named school partner
- Documented student outcomes (even 20 students counts)
- 501(c)(3) status or fiscal sponsor
- Working platform (stable, no critical bugs)

**MathPivot summer program is the proof of concept.**
Document everything: student names (anonymized), hours competed,
accuracy rates before/after, teacher feedback.

### Strategy 2 — Fiscal Sponsorship Bridge
While 501(c)(3) is pending, partner with an existing NC nonprofit
to receive grants on Mpingo's behalf.

Target fiscal sponsors:
- NC Community Foundation (statewide umbrella)
- A Charlotte charter school's parent nonprofit
- UNCF Charlotte chapter
- Local STEM nonprofit (e.g., Code the Dream NC)

### Strategy 3 — Volunteer Pipeline First
For invitation-only corporate sponsors (Lenovo, TI, Red Hat),
the entry point is employee volunteers — not grant applications.

Recruit corporate engineers and data scientists as:
- Competition judges
- Question reviewers
- Curriculum consultants
- Event volunteers

Each volunteer hour unlocks matching grants automatically:
- TI: $125 per 10 hours → $1,000 per 80 hours
- Red Hat: $10/hour up to $1,000/year

### Strategy 4 — Standards-First Narrative
Every proposal leads with the same core argument:

> "39% of 4th graders and 28% of 8th graders perform at or above
> NAEP Proficient level in math. MathAthlone addresses this gap by
> making NC Standard Course of Study content competitive,
> engaging, and identity-affirming for students who don't see
> themselves as 'math kids.'"

Attach NC SCS alignment table to every application.

### Strategy 5 — Diversified Funding Base
No single sponsor exceeds 10% of total contributed revenue.
This protects against:
- Leon Levine Foundation cap compliance
- Cisco Foundation independence requirement
- Financial sustainability if one funder withdraws

## Competitive Differentiation for Grant Applications

MathAthlone is fundable because it is different from existing platforms:

| Platform | What it does | What MathAthlone adds |
|---|---|---|
| Khan Academy | Self-paced practice | Real-time competition, ELO stakes |
| IXL | Adaptive drill | Curriculum-aligned heats, teacher-controlled |
| Prodigy | Game-based math | Standards-aligned, not gamified trivia |
| MATHCOUNTS | Elite competition | Accessible to ALL students, not just gifted |
| Desmos/Amplify | Curriculum tools | Competition layer, engagement motivation |

**Key grant narrative:** MathAthlone is the first platform that makes
NC curriculum-aligned math *competitive for every student* — not just
the ones who already like math.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| 501(c)(3) delayed | Fiscal sponsor in place as backup |
| No pilot school yet | MathPivot summer program as proof of concept |
| Single-funder dependency | Diversified portfolio, no sponsor >10% |
| Platform instability during pilot | Complete bug fixes before any grant application |
| Reputational risk from sponsor | Exclusion list strictly enforced (no defense, utilities, litigation) |
