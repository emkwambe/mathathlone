```
│   (Kahoot/Blooket)    ──► (No competitor) ──►  (MATHCOUNTS│

│                                                           │
│              MathAthlone spans ALL THREE                   │
│                                                           │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**This is both a challenge and an opportunity.** The challenge: schools don't have a budget line item for "math league platform." The opportunity: MathAthlone replaces multiple purchases (quiz tool + competition registration + analytics platform).

## 3. Pricing Principles

### 3.1 Non-negotiable requirements

1. **Free tier must be genuinely useful** — every successful EdTech product has one. Teachers adopt tools they love, then ask their school to pay. A crippled free tier kills adoption.

2. **Per-teacher pricing for individual adoption** — teachers control their own professional development budget ($50–200/year discretionary). Per-student pricing requires admin approval.

3. **Per-student pricing only at school/district scale** — when a principal or curriculum director buys, they think per-student because that's how budgets work.

4. **Tournament revenue is separate** — competition entry fees are a different budget (athletics/extracurricular), not instructional technology.

5. **Price anchors against what it replaces, not what it costs** — MathAthlone replaces Kahoot ($15/teacher/mo) + MATHCOUNTS registration ($45/student) + worksheet prep time (hours/week). The replacement value is high.

### 3.2 Pricing psychology

- **$0 → $1 is the hardest jump.** Free tier must create so much value that upgrading feels obvious.
- **Annual billing earns loyalty.** Monthly churn in EdTech is brutal (summer = mass cancellation). Annual plans with school-year alignment (Aug–Jul) reduce churn.
- **Title I / high-poverty discount is mandatory.** Both ethically and strategically — these schools have the most to gain and the tightest budgets. MATHCOUNTS offers 50% off. MathAthlone should match or exceed this.
- **Pricing should feel "fair" to a teacher spending their own money.** $5–10/mo is the sweet spot. $25/mo feels corporate.

## 4. Recommended Pricing Model

### 4.1 Tier structure

| Tier | Price | Target buyer | What they get |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 forever | Individual teacher trying it out | 3 classrooms, 30 students/class, basic Heats (Practice + School level), CTA scoring, basic results, NC Math 1 content |
| **Pro** | $7/mo or $59/yr | Teacher paying with own budget | Unlimited classrooms/students, all integrity levels, league standings, analytics dashboard, CSV export, all content packs, Class vs Class mode |
| **School** | $4/student/yr (min $500) | Principal or math department head | Whole-school license, unlimited teachers, school-wide leagues, district competition access, admin dashboard, SSO, priority support |
| **District** | $2.50/student/yr (min $2,500) | Curriculum director | All schools in district, district-wide brackets + standings, cross-school leagues, dedicated onboarding, API access, custom reporting |

### 4.2 Why these numbers

**Free → Pro at $7/mo ($59/yr):**
- Below Kahoot's useful tier ($10.50/mo for education)
- Below IXL's single-subject ($9.95/mo)
- Above Blooket Plus ($2.99/mo annual) — justified by league system, ELO, integrity
- $59/yr is well within teacher discretionary budget
- At $7/mo, a teacher with 30 students generates $0.23/student/month in revenue — unsustainable alone, but this tier exists for adoption, not profit

**School at $4/student/yr:**
- Below IXL school pricing (~$5/student)
- Below DreamBox ($5–15/student)
- Above MOEMS ($3.69/student for 5 contests)
- At a 500-student school: $2,000/yr — well within a single line item
- At a 1,000-student school: $4,000/yr — comparable to one Kahoot school license

**District at $2.50/student/yr:**
- Standard volume discount (37.5% off school rate)
- At 5,000 students: $12,500/yr
- At 20,000 students: $50,000/yr
- Comparable to IXL district contracts

### 4.3 Tournament / event pricing (separate revenue stream)

| Event | Fee | Who pays | Budget source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly challenge | Free | — | Engagement driver |
| District qualifier | $25/team (4 students) | School | Academic enrichment / competition |
| Regional championship | $50/team | School | Academic enrichment / competition |
| State championship | $100/team | School | Academic enrichment / competition |
| National championship | Invite-only (sponsored) | Sponsors | Corporate sponsorship |

**Key insight:** Tournament fees come from a DIFFERENT budget than software subscriptions. Academic competition budgets are separate from instructional technology budgets. This means tournament revenue doesn't cannibalize subscription revenue.

Tournament pricing is lower than MATHCOUNTS ($40–45/student) because MathAthlone hosts virtually (no venue costs). The $25/team = $6.25/student for District is aggressively affordable.

### 4.4 Title I / high-need pricing

| Tier | Standard | Title I discount |
|---|---|---|
| Pro (teacher) | $59/yr | $29/yr (50% off) |
| School | $4/student/yr | $2/student/yr (50% off) |
| District | $2.50/student/yr | $1.25/student/yr (50% off) |
| Tournament entry | Standard | Free (sponsor-subsidized) |

Verification: Teacher self-reports Title I status at signup. School/district verification via NCES database lookup.

## 7. Pricing Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Free tier too generous → low conversion | Medium | Gate league features (brackets, ELO, cross-school) behind Pro. Free = practice + school-internal only |
| $7/mo too low for sustainability | Low | Pro tier is adoption, not profit. School/District tiers carry the business |
| Schools won't pay without pilot data | High | Offer 60-day school pilot (full features, no commitment). Convert with data |
| Teachers cancel over summer | High | Annual-only billing aligned to school year (Aug–Jul). No monthly option for School tier |
| District sales cycle too long (6–12 months) | High | Focus Year 1 on teacher adoption + school sales. Districts come from bottom-up demand |
| MATHCOUNTS sees MathAthlone as competitor | Medium | Position as complementary pipeline: "MathAthlone builds the bench. MATHCOUNTS selects the team." |
| Kahoot adds math-specific features | Low | Kahoot is general-purpose. Deep math + league + integrity is 18+ months of moat |

## 8. Go-to-Market Pricing Rollout

### Phase 1: Adoption (Months 1–3)
- Launch with Free + Pro only
- No school/district tier yet (not ready for enterprise sales)
- Target: 500 free teachers, 25 Pro conversions
- Pricing page on landing page with clear comparison

### Phase 2: School tier (Months 4–6)
- Launch School tier after 50+ active schools have teachers using free/Pro
- Pilot program: 10 schools get 60-day full access
- Build case studies from pilot schools
- Hire/contract part-time sales rep for school outreach

### Phase 3: District + tournaments (Months 7–12)
- Launch District tier with first 2–3 district pilots
- Run first tournament series (Monthly → District → Regional)
- Begin sponsorship outreach with pilot data
- Tournament pricing tested and refined

### Phase 4: Scale (Year 2)
- Full pricing matrix live
- Tournament series established (3 splits, regional→state path)
- First national championship (sponsor-funded)
- API/white-label for large districts

## 9. What NOT to do

1. **Don't price per-student for individual teachers.** Teachers won't count heads and do math. Per-teacher is simpler and feels fair.

2. **Don't offer monthly billing for school/district tiers.** Monthly = summer churn. Annual only, aligned to school year.

3. **Don't charge for tournament participation for free-tier teachers.** The tournament is the viral hook. Free teachers can enter classroom-level competitions. Pro teachers can enter school and above.

4. **Don't discount more than 50% for anyone.** Below 50% signals the product isn't worth the list price.

5. **Don't negotiate custom pricing below the per-student floor ($1.25).** That's the Title I rate. Going lower isn't sustainable.
